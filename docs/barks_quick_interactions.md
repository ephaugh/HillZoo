# Hill Zoo — Quick Interaction Barks

113 barks for the quick interaction system — hallway conversations between full meetings. Shorter, more casual, and faster-paced than core response barks. Every line still encodes a surface read and a subtext read.

---

## Ambush Opening Asks

39 barks. NPC intercepts the player with an urgent request. One sentence max.

---

### Vote Ask — Ideologue

1. "This is the line — your vote determines which side you're actually on."
2. "Tomorrow we find out who believes what they claim to believe."
3. "Floor vote tomorrow — this one separates the serious from the comfortable."

### Vote Ask — Follower

1. "Leadership's calling in markers tomorrow — you with us or not?"
2. "The Whip's expecting our votes tomorrow — I'm assuming you got the message?"
3. "Tomorrow's the tell — party's unified on this and we move as one."

### Vote Ask — Dealmaker

1. "Tomorrow I get my vote and you get to cash in whatever you need later."
2. "I need your vote tomorrow — don't worry, I keep meticulous records."
3. "One vote tomorrow, and I'm very good at returning favors."

### Vote Ask — Opportunist

1. "We're voting tomorrow and this one's close — be on the winning side."
2. "Tomorrow's vote is shaping up to be tight — you want to ride the wave or drown in it?"
3. "The numbers say tomorrow's ours if we hold — don't be the one who breaks."

### Cosponsor Ask — Ideologue

1. "This needs your name on it — not anonymously, publicly — do you believe it or not?"
2. "I need a public partner, not a secret supporter — are you actually in?"
3. "Put your name on something that matters and means something — that's what I'm asking."

### Cosponsor Ask — Follower

1. "Leadership wants visible backing — your name on this shows we're serious."
2. "I'm asking you to be out front with us — leadership needs to see commitment."
3. "My bill needs your name — party unity, visible unity."

### Cosponsor Ask — Dealmaker

1. "I need you attached to this publicly — you'll be remembered as the one who showed up."
2. "Your name on my cosponsor list is a favor I will never forget."
3. "Cosponsorship — public, permanent, and I keep a long memory of who did."

### Cosponsor Ask — Opportunist

1. "This is shaping up to be the bill everyone wants credit for — get in now."
2. "The traction's building on this — first movers get the credit."
3. "This one's going somewhere — put your name on it before it becomes obvious."

### Whip Instruction

1. "You're on this vote — leadership expects your yes, don't disappoint."
2. "This is a whip count — we need you locked, are we locked?"
3. "Leadership directed me to your office — your vote is expected tomorrow, confirmed?"

### Help Request — Ideologue

1. "I need you to talk to the chair about this — only someone they respect could move them."
2. "I need you to lean on the chair — you're one of the few they actually listen to."
3. "Can you get a meeting with the chair and explain why this matters?"

### Help Request — Follower

1. "I need you to use your leadership relationships — get the chair to move on this."
2. "Can you reach out to leadership about this? You have credibility I don't."
3. "I need a word in the right ears — you know who those are."

### Help Request — Dealmaker

1. "I need a favor — real pressure on the chair — and I'll remember who came through."
2. "Get the chair to budge on this for me — I'll owe you one, and I always pay."
3. "I need you to squeeze the chair — call it a favor and we'll settle up later."

### Help Request — Opportunist

1. "If you could move the chair on this, the whole dynamic shifts — you'd be the catalyst."
2. "The chair's the bottleneck — get them moving and this rolls."
3. "Get the chair to say yes and suddenly everyone else moves — big moment if you're the one."

## Quick Question Vote Reads

36 barks. Player asks "how will you vote?" in a hallway. One sentence max.

---

### Supportive — Ideologue

1. "This passes the test — you'll have my vote."
2. "I've looked at this carefully — the answer is yes."
3. "This is the right position and I'm holding it."

### Supportive — Follower

1. "Leadership's got this — I'm locked in behind them."
2. "The party's solid on this — count me."
3. "I checked with leadership — you've got me."

### Supportive — Dealmaker

1. "I don't see a downside for me here — you're solid."
2. "No one's offered me anything better, so your vote has my vote."
3. "Works for me — no complications, no debts, no problems."

### Supportive — Opportunist

1. "This is heading in the right direction — I'm on it for now."
2. "The wind's in your favor — I'll ride it."
3. "Numbers look good — as of today, yes."

### Noncommittal — Ideologue

1. "I'm still deciding if this is what it claims to be."
2. "Depends on whether you can convince me the principle holds."
3. "I need to understand the actual argument — the merits still matter to me."

### Noncommittal — Follower

1. "I haven't gotten clarity from leadership yet — once I do, I'm there."
2. "Waiting on the party line — can't move until I know what we're doing."
3. "The signal hasn't come down — I'll vote with the caucus once it does."

### Noncommittal — Dealmaker

1. "Nothing on the table yet that moves me either way."
2. "What else are you offering? Context changes things."
3. "I'm not opposed — just depends what you're willing to put up."

### Noncommittal — Opportunist

1. "Still too close to call — check in when things tighten."
2. "Could go either way right now — I'm watching the margins."
3. "Not enough signal yet — too early for me to pick a side."

### Resistant — Ideologue

1. "I have fundamental problems with what this actually does — don't count on me."
2. "The principle's flawed — that's not negotiable for me."
3. "This isn't what you're selling it as, and I won't vote for the real thing."

### Resistant — Follower

1. "Leadership's against this — so am I."
2. "The party decided, and that decision didn't go your way."
3. "I checked with the Whip — we're sitting this one out."

### Resistant — Dealmaker

1. "Not unless something changes — and by something, I mean an offer."
2. "You'd have to sweeten this considerably for me to move."
3. "I'm a no unless there's a reason to be anything else."

### Resistant — Opportunist

1. "This is losing traction — I'm not riding a losing bill."
2. "The numbers aren't there and they're getting worse — I'm out."
3. "You're fighting uphill on this one — I back winners."

## "I Heard Something" Reactions

18 barks. NPC reacts to player sharing intelligence. Not filtered by temperament.

---

### Grateful (Warm/Allied, useful intel)

1. "That's the kind of intel that actually changes the board — I owe you one, a real one."
2. "You just saved me from walking into a knife — I won't forget this."
3. "This is exactly the piece I was missing — you're better than than I thought."
4. "I needed to hear that more than you know — seriously, thank you."
5. "You went out of your way for this — that matters to me, it actually matters."

### Appreciative (Neutral, useful intel)

1. "Good intel — I'll tuck that away and see where it lands."
2. "That's the kind of detail that separates the amateurs from the planners."
3. "Thanks for the heads-up — forewarned is forearmed."
4. "Appreciate you bringing that to me directly — it stays between us."

### Dismissive (Cold, or irrelevant intel)

1. "Old news travels fast, doesn't it?"
2. "I appreciate the effort, but I've been hearing whispers about that for weeks."
3. "Is that supposed to surprise me? People talk — usually they're wrong."
4. "Thanks for the thought, but I've heard worse rumors from better sources."

### Alarmed (threat to this NPC, any sentiment)

1. "Wait — who exactly told you this? And how sure are we that they're not bluffing?"
2. "That changes everything — if that's real, I need to move now."
3. "You're not joking, are you? Because if you're joking, that's not funny."
4. "That's not just a rumor, is it? You wouldn't bring me this if it wasn't solid."
5. "How long have you known this? And who else is talking?"

## "How Are You Holding Up?" Responses

20 barks. Late-night check-ins. Not filtered by temperament. Driven by sentiment and stress level.

---

### Warm/Allied — Low Stress

1. "Keeping my head above water and plotting my next move — could be worse."
2. "Not complaining — the game's still fun when you're not losing yet."
3. "Living the dream, more or less — or a reasonable facsimile of it."
4. "Better than I deserve to be, truth be told."

### Warm/Allied — High Stress

1. "Between us? I'm running on fumes and spite right now — not sure how much longer that lasts."
2. "I don't think I'm going to make it through this one the same way I came in — but I'm glad it's you asking."
3. "I'm terrified — everything I built is collapsing and I keep pretending it's fine. You're the first person to actually ask."
4. "I'm still standing, but just barely — some days I don't know if that's enough anymore."

### Neutral — Low Stress

1. "Can't complain — well, I can, but it would take all night and you'd hate me for it."
2. "Surviving — which in this town passes for thriving."
3. "Same as always — too busy to fall apart, too tired to celebrate."

### Neutral — High Stress

1. "I'll manage — always do, might take a few years off my life, but I'll manage."
2. "It's been a week — but that's what the job is, stringing together difficult weeks."
3. "Keeping it together — that's the professional answer. Want the real one?"

### Cold/Hostile — Any Stress

1. "I don't need you checking in on me — we're not that kind of relationship."
2. "I'm fine — and I don't appreciate the implications of that question."
3. "I didn't realize we were close enough for this conversation — we're not."

### Lame Duck — Any Stress

1. "Lost my election and they still haven't thrown me out — turns out being expendable is actually liberating."
2. "I'm a dead member walking and it's the most honest I've felt in years — so, surprisingly well."
3. "My career imploded and I'm still here drinking their coffee — there's a dark comedy to it, once you stop caring."
