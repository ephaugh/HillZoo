## Voice Reference — The Four Temperaments

Every bark must be consistent with these voices. This is the #1 quality criterion.

| Temperament | Voice Rules |
|---|---|
| **Ideologue** | Speaks in moral terms. References principles, beliefs, what's right. Never mentions deals, favors, or political convenience. Uses "believe," "principle," "right," "wrong," "conviction." When saying yes: the cause is just. When saying no: the cause is wrong. They don't negotiate — they judge. |
| **Follower** | References authority constantly. Mentions leadership, the party, the Whip, "what we've been told," institutional hierarchy. Rarely uses "I" for political decisions — says "we" or "the party" or "leadership." When saying yes: someone above approved. When saying no: someone above hasn't. Defers upward. |
| **Dealmaker** | Transactional language. References relationships, favors, history, reciprocity, debts, deposits. When saying yes: they're investing. When saying no: the price isn't right. Talks about what you've done for them and what they might do for you. Keeps score openly. |
| **Opportunist** | References momentum, odds, timing, political winds, cosponsors, public attention. When saying yes: your bill is winning. When saying no: your bill is losing. No principles, no loyalty — just bets. IMPORTANT: Vary the vocabulary. Do not overuse the literal word "momentum" — use synonyms: winds, currents, odds, smart money, the room, trajectory, traction, which way things break, who's lining up. |

## Universal Quality Rules

Every bark across all files must pass these checks:

- [ ] 1–2 sentences maximum. Hard limit.
- [ ] Surface read clearly communicates the response (yes/no/maybe/hostile).
- [ ] Subtext encodes which dominant driver caused the response OR which temperament is speaking.
- [ ] Temperament voice is clean. An Ideologue never sounds transactional. A Follower never sounds principled. A Dealmaker never sounds institutional. An Opportunist never sounds convicted.
- [ ] No references to specific bills, members, species, or policy areas. Barks work regardless of context.
- [ ] Tone: sharp, politically cynical, slightly dark-humored. Politicians who happen to be animals, not cartoon characters.

## Critical Context

"I want to discuss..." delivers a mechanical readout below the bark. The bark signals WILLINGNESS and QUALITY. The readout delivers INFORMATION. Barks must NOT promise to deliver information they can't contain. No "I'll tell you exactly where the votes are" — the bark can't contain a vote count.

Warm/Allied barks convey candor and insider access. Neutral barks hedge. Cold barks dismiss.

## Critical Rule

Arm-twist barks encode two signals: the **dominant driver** (WHAT pressure) and the **temperament** (WHO). The driver determines WHAT the NPC talks about. The temperament determines HOW. When temperament overwrites the driver signal, the bark lies about which offer will work.

1. "What's the party line on this one?"
2. "Is leadership backing this? Because I follow where we go as a caucus."

**Problem:** All reference leadership instructions, signaling Party Pressure. Interest Alignment means the bill's CONTENT matters. A Follower asking about content frames it through party identity (does the policy match the platform?), not through institutional instruction (what did the Whip say?).

**Replace all 3. Write to this standard:**
- "Does this bill match what our party has always stood for?"

## Pool 1: Ambush Opening Asks (39 barks)

An NPC intercepts the player with an urgent request. Each bark communicates: what they want, that it's urgent, and a temperament signal. One sentence max.

### Structure

**Vote Ask** — 4 temperaments × 3 variants = 12 barks
The NPC needs the player's vote on an imminent floor vote.
- Ideologue: frames vote as moral obligation
- Follower: frames as institutional duty, references leadership
- Dealmaker: frames as transaction, references future reciprocity
- Opportunist: frames as smart bet, references momentum/numbers

**Cosponsor Ask** — 4 temperaments × 3 variants = 12 barks
Higher stakes than vote — public commitment requested.

**Whip Instruction** — 3 variants (universal, Whip character only)
Direct, authoritative orders. "The Whip expects you to vote YES on Bill 9."

**Help Request** — 4 temperaments × 3 variants = 12 barks
Needs a favor: pressure a chair, introduction, use of influence.

### Examples

Vote — Ideologue: "This bill matters. Your vote matters. Tomorrow."
Vote — Follower: "Leadership needs us on this one. Tomorrow's vote. Are you in?"
Vote — Dealmaker: "I need your vote tomorrow. I'll remember who showed up."

## Pool 2: Quick Question Vote Reads (36 barks)

Player asks "how will you vote?" in a hallway. Brief, casual, one sentence max. No mechanical readout — the player reads the bark to determine the answer.

### Structure

3 tiers × 4 temperaments × 3 variants = 36 barks

| Net Willingness | Tier |
|---|---|
| +20 or higher | Supportive |
| -19 to +19 | Noncommittal |
| -20 or lower | Resistant |

**Supportive:** Signal yes, but temperament colors reliability:
- Ideologue = principled, reliable
- Follower = institutional, reliable IF leadership holds
- Dealmaker = no objection, reliable IF no better offer
- Opportunist = currently favorable, UNRELIABLE if winds shift (include subtle caveats: "for now," "count me in at the moment")

**Noncommittal:** Signal undecided, temperament reveals what would tip them:
- Ideologue = still evaluating merits (amend the bill)
- Follower = waiting for leadership signal (get Whip backing)
- Dealmaker = waiting for an offer (make a deal)
- Opportunist = waiting to see which way things break (build momentum)

**Resistant:** Signal no, temperament reveals why:
- Ideologue = content is wrong
- Follower = party is against it
- Dealmaker = nobody's made it worth their while
- Opportunist = bill is losing

Supportive — Dealmaker: "I've got no reason to block this. You're good."

Noncommittal — Follower: "Waiting on the official word. Can't say yet."
Noncommittal — Dealmaker: "Depends on what else is on the table that day."
Resistant — Ideologue: "I have serious problems with this. Don't count on me."
Resistant — Opportunist: "This doesn't have the votes. I'm not going down with it."

Lingerer-only. Late-night personal check-in. NOT driven by Decision Engine. Driven by sentiment + stress level. NOT filtered by temperament — emotional state overrides personality.

### Structure

| Warm/Allied | High (primary close, bill in danger) | 4 |
| Neutral | Low | 3 |
| Neutral | High | 3 |
| Cold/Hostile | Any | 3 |
| Lame duck | Any | 3 |

### Voice Direction

**Warm + Low:** Relaxed, candid, light humor. A friend unwinding.
**Warm + High:** Vulnerable, honest, grateful. The political mask drops. THESE ARE THE MOST EMOTIONALLY RESONANT BARKS IN THE GAME. Don't rush them.
**Neutral + Low:** Polite, brief, deflects.
**Neutral + High:** Guarded, admits pressure, doesn't open up.
**Cold:** Brushes you off. Why are you even asking?
