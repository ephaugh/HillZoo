# Hill Zoo — Discussion Barks

45 barks for the "I want to discuss..." verb. No political cost. Quality scales with sentiment tier: warm NPCs share real intelligence, neutral NPCs hedge, cold NPCs waste your time.

---

## Discussing a Policy Area

### Discussing a Policy Area — Warm/Allied

1. "Pull up a chair — the version of this issue you've been hearing is about half true."
2. "You want the real picture? Fine. But only because I actually like you."
3. "I've been in this fight longer than most people remember. Ask me anything."
4. "There's a reason nobody talks about this issue honestly. I'm going to."
5. "You came to the right office. Most people in this building will waste your time on this — I won't."

### Discussing a Policy Area — Neutral

1. "It's a complex issue. Lots of perspectives, as you'd expect."
2. "People have strong feelings about it. I'll let the record speak for itself."
3. "That's one of those areas where the chamber's pretty divided. Natural disagreement, really."
4. "Hard to say which way the wind's blowing. Depends who you ask, I suppose."
5. "It's important work, whatever angle you're coming from. Can't say much beyond that."

### Discussing a Policy Area — Cold/Hostile

1. "Not really my area. You'd have to ask around."
2. "Why do you care what I think? Go find your own answers."
3. "That's between me and my conscience. Assuming I have one."
4. "Waste of time discussing it. Nothing changes anyway."
5. "I'm not going to sit here and spell out my positions for you. That's what press releases are for."

## Discussing a Specific Bill

### Discussing a Specific Bill — Warm/Allied

1. "I've read that bill three times and each time I found something new buried in it."
2. "Everyone's got an opinion on that one. Mine happens to be informed."
3. "You should hear what's actually in that bill before you decide how you feel about it."
4. "That legislation has a public face and a private one. I know both."
5. "Smart of you to ask before committing. Most people don't bother."

### Discussing a Specific Bill — Neutral

1. "It's got some merit, I suppose. Also some concerns. Pretty balanced overall, I'd say."
2. "The sponsors seem earnest about it. Time will tell if it actually works."
3. "There's text I agree with and text I don't. Like most bills, really."
4. "It's been through enough drafts that nobody remembers the original intent anymore."
5. "Respectable piece of legislation. Could go either way in committee."

### Discussing a Specific Bill — Cold/Hostile

1. "Don't ask me to read your homework for you."
2. "It's a bill. They all look the same after a while."
3. "I'm not here to do free consulting. Next question."
4. "Ask the sponsor. I'm sure they'll tell you exactly what you want to hear."
5. "Not interested in walking through the fine print with you. Sorry."

## Discussing Another Member

### Discussing Another Member — Warm/Allied

1. "I've served with them long enough to know the difference between the act and the person."
2. "You want my honest read? Close the door first."
3. "That member is more complicated than their reputation suggests. Let me explain."
4. "I've watched them operate for years now. There are patterns worth knowing about."
5. "Most people read them wrong. I'll tell you what to actually watch for."

### Discussing Another Member — Neutral

1. "Seems competent enough. Keeps to themselves mostly. Can't complain."
2. "They've got their thing, I've got mine. We get along fine when our interests align."
3. "Decent member, from what I can tell. Hard to know anybody's real motivations in here."
4. "They're professional, I'll give them that. Beyond that, who knows?"
5. "We don't socialize much, but I've got no beef with them."

### Discussing Another Member — Cold/Hostile

1. "Not my problem what they're up to."
2. "Ask them yourself if you want to know about them."
3. "I'm not going to sit here and gossip about colleagues. That's beneath me."
4. "You seem interested in them. Maybe you should be talking to them instead."
5. "I don't have much to say. And neither should you."
